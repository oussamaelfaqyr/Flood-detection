Emergency Procedures

Follow the steps below for various emergency situations to ensure safety and prompt response.

Fire Emergency

  • Sound the fire alarm immediately if you detect a fire.
  • Evacuate the building quickly, using the nearest exit, avoiding elevators.
  • Once outside, move to the designated assembly point and await further instructions.
  • If caught in smoke, stay low to the ground and cover your nose and mouth with a cloth.

Medical Emergency

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Provide first aid if you are trained, including CPR if needed.
  • Ensure the patient is in a safe position and monitor their condition until help arrives.

Earthquake Emergency

  • If indoors, take cover under a sturdy table or desk, or move to an interior wall.
  • If outdoors, move away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • After the shaking stops, evacuate carefully, checking for hazards like gas leaks or electrical issues.

Other Natural Disasters

  • Stay informed through local emergency services and alerts.
  • Follow instructions for evacuation or shelter, depending on the type of disaster.
  • Have an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, flashlight, and first aid supplies.

Electrical Emergency

  • If there is an electrical fire, use a Class C fire extinguisher or turn off the power if safe to do so.
  • Avoid touching electrical equipment or outlets if you suspect a short circuit or malfunction.
  • In case of an electric shock, call emergency services immediately and do not touch the victim without turning off the power first.
